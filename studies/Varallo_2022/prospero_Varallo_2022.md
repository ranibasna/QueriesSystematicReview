## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

## Review title

Preoperative and perioperative sleep disturbances as risk factors for chronic post-surgical pain: a systematic review and meta-analysis

## Review objectives

The review question will be: are preoperative and perioperative sleep disturbances risk factors for chronic post surgical pain in patients undergoing surgical interventions?

The PEO (Patients, Exposure, Outcome) of this review will be as follows:

Patients: Patients undergoing any type of surgical intervention

Exposure: Preoperative and perioperative sleep disturbances, sleep quality, sleep quantity. Outcome: Presence of chronic post surgical pain; chronic post surgical pain intensity; analgesic use

## Keywords

chronic pain, postoperative pain, predictors, risk factors, Sleep, Sleep disturbances

## Study design

We will include only longitudinal prospective studies. Other inclusion criteria will be: assessment of pain intensity, presence of pain and\\or analgesic use at follow-up after at least 3 months from the surgical intervention; assessment of sleep disturbances using polysomnography, clinical diagnosis or validated questionnaires during the preoperative and perioperative period. Exclusion criteria:

Study reported in languages different from English, Italian, Spanish, German, Portuguese. No restriction will be placed on publication date.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

The International Association for the Study of Pain defines persistent postoperative pain as a clinical discomfort that lasts more than 3 months after surgery without other causes of pain, such as chronic infection or pain from a chronic condition that preceded the surgery. Chronic post-surgical pain can represent a severe impediment to patients, leading to functional limitation and psychological distress.

Persistent pain following surgical procedures is a well-known and costly issue. Several risk factors have been identified, such as preoperative pain, psychosocial factors, and specific surgical procedures. Nonetheless, recent evidence suggests that sleep disturbance might also aggravate post-operative pain. To date, no review has systematically investigated the role of preoperative and perioperative sleep disturbance as a risk factor for chronic postoperative pain.

## Population

Inclusion criteria: adult patients undergoing any type of surgery. Exclusion criteria: adolescent patients (i.e. age &lt;18).

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

Presence of sleep disturbances assessed using polysomnography or clinically-diagnosed sleep disorders (e.g. insomnia, hypersomnia, narcolepsy, OSA).

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

None

## Context

We will include studies from all countries and from all socio-economic categories.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

We will include studies measuring pain as a continuous variable (i.e. pain intensity) or measured as a dichotomous variable (0=no presence of chronic post surgical pain, 1= presence of chronic post surgical pain). All the outcomes should have been measured at least three months after surgery.

## Measures of effect

We will extract correlations coefficients, unstandardized and/or standardized regression coefficients, raw mean values and SDs, t-tests, odds ratios, or risk ratios representing the association between preoperative and postoperative sleep disturbances and pain at all the follow-up evaluations.

## Additional outcomes

The secondary outcome will be analgesic use.

## Measures of effect

We will extract data regarding: number or percentage of patients using analgesics of any type at follow-up, number or percentage of patients using opioid analgesic drugs at follow-up.

The quality of the included studies was assessed using the Quality In Prognosis Studies (QUIPS) tool. This tool was chosen for its specificity to assess risk of bias of studies focusing on health-related predictors. ## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

Firstly, a narrative synthesis of the results of the included articles will be performed. This procedure will be performed to assess the consistency regarding the association of sleep disturbances with pain, while also considering the methodological quality of the corresponding studies.

Then, if random-effects meta-analyses will be performed separately according to timing of the assessment of sleep disorders (i.e. pre-operative or peri-operative) and outcome (pain or analgesic use). These analyses will be performed only if at least three effect sizes representing the relationship between the predictor and the outcome could be extracted from the original studies.

All the effect sizes extracted from the original studies will be firstly converted to Pearson's r using standard formulae, and then converted to Fisher's z to be pooled. The result of each meta-analysis will be finally back-transformed to Pearson's r for a more immediate interpretation. When both bivariate and multivariable analyses will be present, estimates of the former will be included in the analysis. Data from different time points, subscales (e.g. the subscales of the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index), outcome measurement instruments (e.g. VAS and NRS) or subgroups (e.g. primary knee arthroplasty and revision knee arthroplasty) within a single study, if comparable, will be aggregated using standard formulae. Estimates from data points distanced more than 12 months will be analyzed separately. Missing data will be requested from study authors. In case there will be missing data left after requesting them from the study authors, we will consider using a multiple imputation technique to account for them. Heterogeneity will be assessed using the I² index. Mixed-model meta-analyses will be then performed to determine sources of heterogeneity. All fixed factors will be included in a first mixed-effect model and then deleted through a backward procedure until only significant factors will remain.

## Analysis of subgroups or subsets

Subgroup analyses will be conducted based on types of sleep disturbances, surgery type, and outcome measures (chronic post surgical pain intensity or presence of chronic post surgical pain).

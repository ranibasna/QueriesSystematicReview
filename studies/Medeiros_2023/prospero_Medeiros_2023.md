## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

When children do not sleep for the National Sleep Foundation recommended hours, sleep restriction can be a trigger for the development of problems such as impaired cognition, behavioral issues, cardiovascular problems, and obesity. Besides, the inflammatory profile of children may also be influenced by sleep restriction and/or obesity.

## Population

Children ≥ 2 years and adolescent until 19 years.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

Sleep duration is derived based on the number of hours slept per night. In the United States, national data have shown a decline in sleep duration among newborns to 10-year-olds over the past 50 years by 1.5-2 h which may be attributable to changes in lifestyle, such as waking up early for school and late night activities.14, 15 Sleep disturbance, characterized by disruptions in quantity, quality, or timing of sleep, frequently occur in children. In Europe, a comprehensive study found a median of daily sleep of 10 h, with normal weight children sleeping on average 20 min per day more than overweight children.

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

Children with adequate sleep time

## Context

The inclusion criteria were as follows: i) the study population was composed of children and/or adolescents according to the World Health Organization (WHO) classification, ii) the study was original research, iii) the study was performed with at least one subjective measurement (questionnaire) or an objective measurement (an accelerometer, i.e., an Ambulatory Monitoring Inc (AMI) device, Mini-Mitter device, Cambridge device, or other device) for sleep time, and iv) the study reported some relation with sleep time and inflammation. Studies in which the participants had different diseases (or disturbances) that could interfere with sleep time were excluded. These criteria were set to increase inter-study comparability. In addition, review articles or books and studies with subjects outside the age range (3-19 y), and studies there no analyzed sleep time with inflammation were excluded.

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Studies will be selected according to the following steps: (1) articles published in English, Spanish, and Portuguese; (2) titles of the articles will be screened; (3) abstracts will be screened; and (4) the full texts of the articles will be reviewed to determine whether they meet the inclusion criteria. The selection and data extraction phases will be executed by two reviewers independently. Discrepancies will be resolved through discussion. Data extraction from retrieved articles, to assess risk of bias and for the descriptive sythesis will be carried out using a standardized form, which will include:

- 1) Author names and publication date;
- 2) Study period (follow-up period, in longitudinal studies);
- 3) Study name/title;
- 4) Geographic region(s) where the study was conducted;
- 5) Sampling method;
- 6) Sample size;
- 7) Age and sex of subjects;
- 8) Study design;
- 9) Method for plasma inflammatory biomarker dosage;
- 10) Sleep assessment tool;
- 11) Method to assess the association;
- 12) Confounding variables;
- 13) Conflict of interest statements;
- 14) Main findings;

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

We will have two independents researches and two researches to evaluate some conflict. All retrieved articles will critically appraise the articles using the STROBE check-list to identify sources of bias, performance, attrition, and detection. Data relevant to this review will include the study design, characteristics of the research subjects, methods for sleep assessment used, and results.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

Analyze sleep time using questionnaires and accelerometers and the relationship between sleep time in order to find a dichotomous or continuous outcome, so we intend to use a descriptive synthesis of sleep time association with inflammation (inflammation being represented by higher plasma C-reactive protein, interleukin-6 and TNF-glyph&lt;c=0,font=/CAAAAA+ArialMT&gt; and lower plasma adiponectin concentration) in a table format.

We intend to investigate the possibility (based on number of studies, common outcomes and homogenety) of conducting a metanalysis for the association by sex, age group 3 to 10 years for children and 11 to 19 years for teenagers and nutritional status through the body mass index.

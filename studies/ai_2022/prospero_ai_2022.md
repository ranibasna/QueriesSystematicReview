## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

## Review title

Blood pressure in children and adolescents with sleep-disordered breathing: a systematic review and meta-analysis

## Review objectives

Do children and adolescents with sleep-disordered breathing (SDB) have alteration in blood pressure?

## SEARCHING AND SCREENING

## Searches

We will search three databases, including MEDLINE, Web of Science, and PubMed for eligible studies. There were no restrictions for the date of publication, while literature search was limited to the English.

## Study design

Case-control, cross-sectional, and longitudinal cohort studies that compared the blood pressure parameters (e.g., systolic/diastolic blood pressure during sleep or awake, BP load, and nondipping) of children and adolescents that had a spectrum of sleep-disordered breathing disorders with healthy controls.

We will include studies that have evaluated blood pressure in children or adolescents with SDB and compared them with healthy controls. The exclusion criteria will be:

- (a) Lack of controls (healthy controls);
- (b) Performed research without measurement of blood pressure;
- (c) Recruited children had other pathological condition (i.e., congenital heart disease) beyond SDB;
- (d) Blood pressure measures were performed after an intervention.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

Blood pressure. Sleep-disordered breathing (SDB) in children.

## Population

Children and adolescents with SDB, without clinical history of congenital heart disease and without any surgical or CPAP intervention for SDB.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

There were no interventions, and only case-control, cross-sectional, and longitudinal cohort studies will be included in this review. In this review, we aim to study the changes of blood pressure in SDB children and adolescents. The main exposure was children and adolescents with a spectrum of SDB (i.e., primary snoring, mild, moderate, or severe SDB) aged from 0 to 18 years old. Different BP measurements, such as armcuff or ambulatory blood pressure (ABP), were adopted to evaluate the changes of blood pressure in SDB children or adolescents.

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

Healthy children, without any sleep disturbance history.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

Mean blood pressure (MBP), systolic and diastolic BP during sleep or awake.

Measures of effect

NA

## Additional outcomes

BP load, nondipping BP, and SBP Index.

## Measures of effect

NA

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Two authors will independently examine the titles, abstracts, and full-text to identify eligible articles. We will also search the reference lists and the review articles for additional studies. In case of disagreement, a third reviewer will be invited to evaluate the article in order to reach an agreement. Duplicate papers, meeting abstracts, letters and editorials will be excluded. In data extraction, two authors will independently extract the data. If there is a disagreement, we will invite a third author to review the data until a consensus is reached. We will include the following data of the identified articles: name of the first author, country (origin) of data collection, year of publication of the study, sample size, groups evaluated (SDB and healthy), age, mean and standard deviation (SD) of BP/SBP/DBP during asleep or awake will be extracted and recorded using a spreadsheet. If raw data are not available in the text, we will look for any additional data or graphical information that could be used for difference calculation. In absence of any available data, we will contact the correspondence author ask for the required data.

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

The Newcastle-Ottawa Scale (NOS;

http://www.ohri.ca/programs/clinical\_epidemiology/oxford.asp) will be used to evaluate the risk of bias for case-control and longitudinal cohort studies. This scale applies a 'star system' to evaluate the quality of case-control and cohort studies by measuring three components: the selection of the study population, the comparability of the study groups, and the ascertainment of exposure or outcome. The NOS scores were converted into 'good,' 'fair,' and 'poor' quality following Agency for Research and Quality standards. AXIS tool will be used for the assessment of cross-sectional studies (https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/6/12/e011458).

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

Data synthesis will be performed when at least 3 studies per outcome are found. For all outcome variables, Hedges' g will be calculated using between group (SDB vs control or primary snoring) values of mean, standard deviation and sample size, and presented with 95% confidence intervals. Under the assumption of variability of true effects between studies, a random-effects meta-analysis model will be used. I² test will be used to assess heterogeneity, where values above 50% and 75% will be considered indicative of moderate and high heterogeneity respectively.

## Analysis of subgroups or subsets

The groups to be studied are children and adolescents with a clinical diagnosis of SDB as compared to healthy individuals.We anticipate subgroup analysis based on different age groups (children vs adolescents), measurements (armcuff vs ambulatory blood pressure), and severity of SDB (i.e., primary snoring vs healthy; mild SDB vs helathy; moderate SDB vs healthy; severe SDB vs healthy).

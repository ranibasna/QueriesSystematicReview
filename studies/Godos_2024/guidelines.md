# PROSPERO Registration — Sleep Apnea and Dementia
**Title:** The Association Between Sleep Apnea and Dementia: A Systematic Review  
**Registration ID:** CRD42021233096  
**Citation:** Martin Guay‑Gagnon; Philippe Desmarais. *The Association Between Sleep Apnea and Dementia: A Systematic Review.* PROSPERO 2024.  
**Record URL:** https://www.crd.york.ac.uk/PROSPERO/view/CRD42021233096

## Review objectives
Assess the evidence for an association between sleep apnea and the incidence of dementia (all‑cause) and specific dementia phenotypes.

## Keywords
Alzheimer disease; Biomarker; Dementia; Frontotemporal lobar degeneration; Lewy body disease; Neurocognitive disorder; Parkinson's disease; Sleep apnea; Vascular dementia

## Information sources and search
- **Database:** Web of Science Core Collection  
- **Coverage:** Inception to **1 March 2021**  
- **Language:** No restriction

## Eligibility criteria
- **Designs:** Prospective or retrospective cohort studies; randomized controlled trials (if evaluating incidence).  
- **Population:** Patients with a diagnosis of sleep apnea confirmed by polysomnography (PSG) or overnight oximetry; AHI/ODI thresholds must be specified. No age limit.  
- **Exposure:** Presence of sleep apnea.  
- **Comparator:** Patients without sleep apnea.  
- **Outcomes:** Onset of dementia (all-cause) and phenotypes (Alzheimer’s disease, Parkinson’s disease dementia, Lewy body dementia, frontotemporal dementia and related variants, vascular dementia, mixed dementia).  
- **Notes:** Studies lacking clear sleep‑apnea diagnostic criteria are excluded.

## Data extraction (selection and coding)
Two independent reviewers extract data; disagreements resolved by consensus. Planned data elements include: author, year, design, country, N, sleep‑apnea diagnostic criteria and thresholds (AHI/ODI), AHI/ODI means and categorical cut‑offs (>15, >30), CPAP/BPAP treatment counts, sex %, BMI, age at SA diagnosis and baseline, incident dementia cases and subtype, age at dementia, diagnostic criteria, biomarker use, follow‑up, adjusted covariates. Extraction via a standardized, pilot‑tested Excel form.

## Risk of bias / quality assessment
- **Observational studies:** Newcastle–Ottawa Scale (selection, comparability, outcome domains).  
- **Randomized trials:** Cochrane RoB 2 tool.  
Assessments by two independent reviewers with consensus resolution.

## Planned synthesis
Quantitative and qualitative synthesis of the association between sleep apnea and dementia; meta‑analysis contingent on sufficiency of included studies.

## Subgroup analyses
- Moderate–severe sleep apnea (AHI or ODI > 15)  
- Severe sleep apnea (AHI or ODI > 30)  
- Patients treated with positive pressure ventilation (CPAP or BPAP)  
Effect measure: Relative risk.

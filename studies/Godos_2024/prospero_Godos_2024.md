## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

## Review title

A comprehensive systematic review on the association between Mediterranean diet and its components and sleep features in adults: evidence from observational studies

## Review objectives

This study aims to investigate how adherence to the Mediterranean diet and its components intake may be related to sleep features in adult population.

## SEARCHING AND SCREENING

## Searches

A systematic search on PubMed, EMBASE, and Web of Science for all studies examining the relation between Mediterranean diet and/or its components and diverse sleep features will be performed from their inception. The search strategy will be based on the combination of the relevant keywords related to Mediterranean diet and its components (such as fruit, vegetable, cereals, legumes, dairy products, fish, meat, olive oil and alcohol) and sleep. Reference lists of eligible studies will also be screened for any additional studies not previously identified. The systematic search and study selection will be performed by two independent authors. The design and reporting of this study will follow the Meta-analyses Of Observational Studies in Epidemiology (MOOSE) guidelines.

## Study design

Observational studies with a comparison group (cohort studies, cross-sectional studies, casecontrol studies)

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

Sleep features

## Population

Inclusion criteria: Adults (≥18 years old), both gender, and all ethnicities.

Exclusion criteria: Children or adolescents (&lt;18 years old), pregnant women, and patients with end-stage degenerative diseases.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

Adherence to the Mediterranean diet and/or its components intake

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

Different levels of adherence to the Mediterranean diet or different categories of its components intake

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

Sleep features (i.e., sleep quality, sleep duration, daytime sleepiness etc.)

## Additional outcomes

None

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Data from all eligible studies will be extracted using a standardized electronic form. The following information will be extracted: first author name, publication year, study design and location, population age and gender, sample size, details on the assessment method and adherence to the Mediterranean diet, details of the assessment method and intake of Mediterranean diet components, details on the assessment method of the outcome of interest, and main findings of the study. Two investigators will extract the data and assess the methodological quality independently and any incongruity will be resolved by the consensus.

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

The quality of each included study will be assessed according to the Newcastle-Ottawa Quality Assessment Scale (NOS).

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

The study will provide a narrative overview and critical analysis of the retrieved observational studies evaluating the link between adherence to the Mediterranean diet and/or its components intake and diverse sleep features in adult population.

## Analysis of subgroups or subsets

Not applicable

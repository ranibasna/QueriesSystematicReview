## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

## Review title

Sleep quality and architecture in genetic generalised epilepsy: A systematic review and metaanalysis

## Review objectives

Is there evidence of impaired sleep quality and altered sleep architecture in children, adolescents and young adults with genetic generalised epilepsy (GGE) compared to healthy controls?

Additional questions:

- 1) Are there differences in quality of sleep and sleep architecture between the four GGE syndromes?
- 2) Are there differences in quality of sleep and sleep architecture between drug-naïve and antiseizure-drug-treated patients?

## SEARCHING AND SCREENING

## Searches

- - Systematic search of databases: MEDLINE, EMBASE, Scopus
- - In addition, we will search reference lists of eligible studies

## Study design

Cohort studies and cross-sectional studies

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

Genetic/idiopathic generalised epilepsy. Sleep quality and sleep architecture.

## Population

Inclusion: Individuals with genetic/idiopathic generalised epilepsy (as defined by ILAE criteria; including Juvenile myoclonic epilepsy (JME), Childhood absence epilepsy (CAE), Juvenile absence epilepsy (JAE), GGE with Generalised tonic-clonic seizures (GTCS)) Exclusion: Participants of case group suffering from other neurological disease.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

Exposure: Genetic/idiopathic generalised epilepsy diagnosis

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

Inclusion: Healthy controls

Exclusion: Participants of control group suffering from other epilepsy form or neurological disease.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

Sleep macro- and microstructure in children, adolescents and young adults with genetic generalised epilepsy (GGE) compared to healthy controls

## Measures of effect

standardized mean difference

## Additional outcomes

- - Scores of used sleep questionnaires in children, adolescents and young adults with genetic generalised epilepsy (GGE) compared to healthy controls
- - Reported sleep disorders in children, adolescents and young adults with genetic generalised epilepsy (GGE) compared to healthy controls

## Measures of effect

summarized qualitatively in a tabulated form

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Study selection:

In a first step, the identification of relevant literature is realized by title &amp; abstract screening. Subsequently, potentially eligible studies are read full text by two reviewers independently and in duplicate. In case of disagreement about meeting the eligibility criteria, studies in question will be discussed with an experienced third reviewer.

Data extraction:

Two reviewers will extract the data independently and in duplicate using a standardised form. In case of disagreement a third reviewer will be consulted.

## Data to be extracted:

- - Sample characteristics (including sample size, Epilepsy diagnosis, Mean Age, Use of antiepileptic drug)
- - Outcome-related items (parameters of sleep macrostructure and sleep microstructure, scores of used sleep questionnaires, reported sleep disorders)

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

For the assessment of quality and risk of bias of the included studies we will consider the STROBE- (Strengthening the reporting of observational studies in Epidemiology) Statement. To evaluate the potential impact of publication bias in the meta-analyses we will use funnel plots and Egger's test.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

For the assessment of the parameters of sleep macro- and microstructure, we will use standardized mean difference (Cohen's d) as effect measure in order to conduct random effect univariate meta-analyses. The results of each analysis will be visually displayed in a forest plot.

The heterogeneity between the included studies will be estimated by the I² statistic.

Results of sleep questionnaires and reported sleep disorders in the individual studies will be summarized qualitatively in a tabulated form.

## Analysis of subgroups or subsets

If sufficient data are available, subtype effect estimates of GGE subtypes will be included.

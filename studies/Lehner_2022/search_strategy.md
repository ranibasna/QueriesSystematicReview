# Search Strategy for Lehner_2022

## Study Summary

- Topic: Sleep quality and sleep architecture in children, adolescents, and young adults with genetic generalised epilepsy
- Date window: No inline publication-date restriction; the protocol gives a review timeline but does not specify a retrieval date boundary
- Databases: pubmed, scopus, wos, embase
- Query level: extended
- Retrieval architecture: single_route
- design_analytic_block: active, but explicit design and age restrictions are used conservatively as later filters rather than baseline Q1 constraints

## PICOS Summary

- Population: Individuals with genetic or idiopathic generalised epilepsy, including juvenile myoclonic epilepsy, childhood absence epilepsy, juvenile absence epilepsy, and GGE with generalized tonic-clonic seizures; children, adolescents, and young adults
- Intervention or Exposure: Genetic or idiopathic generalised epilepsy diagnosis
- Comparator: Healthy controls
- Outcomes: Sleep quality, sleep architecture, sleep macrostructure, sleep microstructure, sleep questionnaire scores, and reported sleep disorders
- Design: Cohort studies and cross-sectional studies

## Architecture Selection Summary

- Selected retrieval architecture: `single_route`
- `design_analytic_block`: active

The protocol defines one coherent Boolean route linking a generalized-epilepsy population block to a sleep-outcome block. It does not justify separate indexed and textword routes for the same mandatory concepts, so `dual_route_union` is not warranted. The protocol's observational-design and younger-age constraints are explicit, but they are recall-risky enough to keep out of baseline Q1 and use in later filters and bundled variants instead.

## Searches

We conducted a literature search utilizing the electronic databases PubMed and EMBASE. The phrases and key words we used were included but were not limited with: 'sleep', 'sleep disorders', 'sleep dysfunction', 'sleep disturbance', 'fertility', 'infertility', 'in vitro fertilization', 'fertility treatment', 'sleep breathing disorder', 'obstructive sleep apnea', 'circadian dysrhythmia', 'melatonin', 'HPG axis', 'HPA axis' and 'oxidate stress' et al from January 1, 2010 to November 1, 2023.

## Study design

We will include observational studies (including cross-sectional studies and prospective cohort studies) for the assessment.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

Instead of just being a life quality concern, infertility is a disease that poses a serious threat to patients' physical and social health. In recent years, the female infertility rate among agestandardized populations has grown by 14.962% globally, and increased by 0.370% per year. Additionally, 25% of Chinese couples constantly attempting pregnancy suffered from infertility. Meanwhile, endocrine disorders such as diabetes, and thyroid disease, polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), advanced paternal and maternal age, underweight and overweight were considered to be risk factors of female infertility. Lifestyle factors such as sleep, diet, smoking and drinking in some way can also negatively influence fertility. Sleep disturbance commonly occurs in infertile patients. However, the studies investigating the sleep patterns in infertile females are currently rare, and the association of sleep disturbance and female infertility is largely remained unknown. In recent years, the relationship between sleep and female infertility has gained more attention than before, as the probable pathways by which sleep alteration may impair female reproductive health and function are gradually being elucidated.

## Population

Studies conducted among reproductive-aged women with infertility and any condition of sleep disturbances will be included. We will include observational and prospective cohort studies that among reproductive-age women, as well as those that have enrolled specific subpopulations such as women undergoing any kind of infertility treatment or with sleep breathing disorders.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

This review aimed to analyze studies that evaluate the association between sleep or any condition of sleep disorders and female infertility.

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

This review concluded studies: 1). sleep disorders on reproductive-age females in different populations. 2). sleep disorders on females receiving IVF treatment. 3). sleep breathing disorder on females with infertility

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

Throughout this review, we intend to explore the impacts of sleep disturbance and various sleep patterns on infertility and review the potential mechanism, furthermore, to assess the causes and better management of sleep disturbance among infertile females.

## Additional outcomes

The association between female infertility and Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) scores.

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Our search strategy identified a total of 1172 articles ， after screening and reevaluating 1089 titles and abstracts, 20 relevant studies focusing on the impact of sleep disturbance on female infertility were finally extracted

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

Quality appraisal will be conducted by two reviewers independently. Disagreements will be resolved by discussion and mutual agreement between the group members.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

The objective of the review is to synthesis evidence on the association between sleep and female infertility, review articles and studies which did not access both sleep or sleep disturbance and female infertility would be excluded. However, articles that describe the association between sleep disturbance and female infertility but do not access the fertility characteristics such as fertility hormone levels, pregnancy outcomes, oocytes and embryos characteristics, self-reported infertility or medical-recorded infertility would also be excluded.

## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

## Review title

Biological rhythms in premenstrual syndrome and premenstrual dysphoric disorder: a systematic review

## Review objectives

Are there biological rhythms disruptions in women with PMDD or PMS?

## SEARCHING AND SCREENING

## Searches

We have conducted a search with no year or language restriction on July 31st, 2019. The search was conducted in the following databases: PubMed, PsycINFO, Embase, Web of Science.

## Study design

We will include cross-sectional and longitudinal studies. Clinical trials providing the participant's baseline biological rhythms measurements can also be included. We will not include case reports, reviews or meta-analysis.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

Symptoms of PMS have been reported to affect up to 90% of women of reproductive age, and nearly 20% of women experience significant PMS, which interferes greatly in their quality of life. PMDD affects 5-9% of women of reproductive age worldwide, and can cause great burden in their productivity and overall functioning, leading to incapacitation in severe cases.

## Population

Our participant population are women in reproductive age with a diagnosis of PMS or PMDD, and healthy controls.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

Our exposition is PMS or PMDD.

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

The control group are women in reproductive age without a diagnosis of PMS or PMDD.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

The main outcome are biological rhythms measurements in women with PMS/PMDD compared with healthy controls.

## Measures of effect

Biological Rhythms measured by scales or hormones.

## Additional outcomes

None.

## Measures of effect

Not applicable

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Articles were extracted from four databases on July 31, 2019 with no year or language restriction, using the following databases: PubMed, Embase, MEDLINE, and Web of Science. The search criteria used was (biological rhythm OR biological rhythms OR circadian rhythm OR circadian rhythms OR sleep disruption OR melatonin) AND (PMDD OR premenstrual dysphoric disorder OR PMS OR premenstrual syndrome OR premenstrual syndromes). This search generated 529 articles (PubMed = 200, Embase = 147, MEDLINE = 104, Web of Science = 78). After removing duplicate articles, we have excluded all articles that were: (1) case reports; (2) reviews or meta-analyses; (3) clinical trials with no relevant baseline reports, (4) studies that did not control for other primary psychiatric disorders. The articles were first screened by title, then by abstract, and finally by full-text. All articles were independently evaluated by two blinded reviewers (AN and LC) to determine if studies met the inclusion criteria, and any discrepancies in the assessments were resolved by consensus.

Data was extracted from the final selection of articles by three researchers (AN, LC, and TC).

Basic information such as the first author, year, and country of research were identified. The type of study design and the aim of the study were extracted with the consideration of the purpose of the review article (for example, though clinical trials studies were generally excluded, those that included baseline measures were included in this review). Population statistics were also extracted, which included the number of participants (healthy controls and experimental sample) as well as their ages. Additionally, methodological data was extracted, such as the instruments used for neuropsychological evaluation, questionnaires, and methods of obtaining samples, such as serum, salivary, or urinary samples. Lastly, the most relevant findings were summarized alongside the conclusion of an association between the control and experimental groups.

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

We will do a bias assessment using the Newcastle Ottawa Scale.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

We are describing the main results found in the included studies that are relevant to the aim of this systematic review. The results are divided by the biological rhythms domains such as sleep (assessed by polysomnography, sleep EEG, melatonin levels and subjective questionnaires on sleep quality), other hormonal levels (TSH, cortisol, prolactin), body temperature, and level of activity.

Considering the heterogeneity of the data found, we are not able to conduct a meta-analysis. There are 3 researchers involved in this process, and discrepancies were solved by consensus.

## REVIEW TITLE AND BASIC DETAILS

## Review title

The Association Between Sleep Apnea and Dementia : A Systematic Review

## Review objectives

The purpose of this systematic review is to assess the evidence for an association between sleep apnea and the incidence of dementia.

## Keywords

Alzheimer disease, Biomarker, Dementia, Frontotemporal lobar degeneration, Lewy body disease, Neurocognitive disorder, Parkinson's disease, Sleep apnea, Vascular dementia

## SEARCHING AND SCREENING

## Searches

The source that will be used for the search is the Web of Science Core Collection databases, from time of inception of the database until March 1st, 2021. There will be no language restriction.

## Study design

Eligible study designs are prospective or retrospective cohort studies and randomized controlled trials.

The population included in these studies must consist of patients with a diagnosis of sleep apnea confirmed with a polysomnography study or overnight oximetry. The outcome measured must be the onset of dementia (all cause) and / or the following phenotypes of dementia : Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease dementia, Lewy body dementia, frontotemporal dementia and related disorders (i.e., behavioral variant frontotemporal dementia, primary progressive aphasia, corticobasal syndrome, and progressive supranuclear palsy syndrome), vascular dementia, mixed dementia.

## ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

## Condition or domain being studied

The diseases of interest are sleep apnea and dementia (all cause) and the following clinical phenotypes of dementia : Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease dementia, Lewy body dementia, frontotemporal dementia and related disorders (i.e., behavioral variant frontotemporal dementia, primary progressive aphasia, corticobasal syndrome, and progressive supranuclear palsy syndrome), vascular dementia, mixed dementia.

## Population

The population being studied consists of patients with sleep apnea diagnosed either by a polysomnography study or an overnight oximetry study. The threshold of apnea-hypopnea index (AHI) or oxygen desaturation index (ODI) must be clearly specified. Studies in which the diagnosis criteria for sleep apnea are not specified or rely on other methods are excluded. There will be no age limit.

## Intervention(s) or exposure(s)

The exposure of interest is the presence of sleep apnea.

## Comparator(s) or control(s)

The control group must consist of patients without sleep apnea.

## OUTCOMES TO BE ANALYSED

## Main outcomes

The main outcome is the onset of dementia (all-cause). There are no inclusion or exclusion criteria regarding the way that the diagnosis is made.

## Measures of effect

The effect measure will be the relative risk of incidental dementia in patients with sleep apnea compared to patients without sleep apnea.

## Additional outcomes

Additional outcomes measured will be the onset of the following clinical phenotypes of dementia : Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease dementia, Lewy body dementia, frontotemporal dementia and related disorders (i.e., behavioral variant frontotemporal dementia, primary progressive aphasia, corticobasal syndrome, and progressive supranuclear palsy syndrome), vascular dementia, mixed dementia.

Use of biomarkers to support the clinical diagnosis will be collected (e.g., structural and functional brain imaging, CSF biomarkers, blood biomarkers, neuropathology, etc.)

## Measures of effect

The effect measure used for the additional outcomes will be relative risks.

## DATA COLLECTION PROCESS

## Data extraction (selection and coding)

Articles will be reviewed and data extracted from included articles independently by two different reviewers according to the pre-specified criteria. Divergences between the reviewers will be resolved by consensus. The data extracted from the articles will be as follows :

- • Study Main Author
- • Year of publication
- • Study design
- • Country from which the study population is from
- • Number of subjects included
- • Number of subjects with sleep apnea
- • Diagnostic criteria for sleep apnea (polysomnography or overnight oximetry)
- · Minimum apnea hypopnea index (AHI) or oxygen desaturation index (ODI) used for the diagnosis
- • Mean AHI or ODI
- • Number of subjects with and AHI or ODI &amp;gt; 15 (moderate to severe apnea)
- • Number of subjects with and AHI or ODI &amp;gt; 30 (severe apnea)
- · Number of patients for which the sleep apnea is treated with positive pression ventilation (CPAP or BPAP)
- • % Male
- • Mean BMI
- • Age at the time of diagnosis of sleep apnea
- • Age at baseline
- • Incidental cases of dementia
- • Clinical phenotype of dementia
- • Age at the time of diagnosis of dementia
- • Diagnostic criteria used for dementia
- • Dementia biomarker, if used
- • Follow-up duration
- • Adjusted variables

The data extracted will be recorded in a standardized, pilot-tested form using Microsoft Excel software.

## Risk of bias (quality) assessment

The quality assessment of each study will be done indenpendently by two reviewers and any divergences will be resolved by consensus.

The quality assessment for observational studies will be done according to the NewcastleOttawa scale for observational studies, which comprises the following elements:

- 1) Representativeness of the exposed cohort
- 2) Selection of the non exposed cohort
- 3) Ascertainment of exposure
- 4) Demonstration that outcome of interest was not present at start of study
- 5) Comparability of cohorts on the basis of the design or analysis
- 6) Assessment of outcome
- 7) Was follow-up long enough for outcomes to occur
- 8) Adequacy of follow-up of cohorts

The quality assessment for randomized controlled trials will be done according to the Cochrane

RoB 2 tool, which is a well validated tool recommended to assess the risk of bias in randomized controlled trials included in Cochrane reviews.

The data extracted will be recorded in a standardized, pilot-tested form using Microsoft Excel software.

## PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS

## Strategy for data synthesis

A quantitative and qualitative synthesis of the data collected will be provided assessing the association between sleep apnea and dementia.

The decision to do a meta-analysis of the data will depend on if there is sufficient studies included in the systematic review for it to be relevant.

## Analysis of subgroups or subsets

Subgroup analysis will be done for :

- • Subjects with moderate to severe sleep apnea (AHI ou ODI &amp;gt; 15)
- • Subjects with severe sleep apnea (AHI or ODI &amp;gt; 30)
- • Subjects who are treated with positive pressure ventilation

Relative risks will be the effect measure used for these subgroups.

### **ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA**

**Condition or domain being studied**
Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic, inflammatory, and demyelinating disease of the central nervous system, leading to a variety of symptoms. Sleep disorders are one of the most underestimated and under-diagnosed symptoms affecting the quality of life in people with MS (PwMS). The most reported sleep disorders in PwMS are insomnia, sleep apnea, restless legs syndrome (RLS), narcolepsy, and rapid eye movement sleep behavior disorders.

**Population**
*   **Inclusion criteria:** People aged ≥18 years people with multiple sclerosis with sleep disorder will be included.
*   **Exclusion criteria:** studies not written in English, non-human participants, studies where randomization protocol were not reported or unclearly reported.

**Intervention(s) or exposure(s)**
Non-pharmacological and non-invasive intervention to improve sleep related outcomes

**Comparator(s) or control(s)**
Comparator of standard care, no treatment, attentional, or wait list control

### **DATA COLLECTION PROCESS**

**Data extraction (selection and coding)**
Two reviewers will separately assess the titles and abstracts of the studies obtained through the searches to determine the inclusion of relevant studies. We will document the selection process meticulously to facilitate the creation of a comprehensive PRISMA flow diagram.

**Risk of bias (quality) assessment**
The risk of bias will be determined via the Physiotherapy Evidence Database (PEDro) scale.

---

### **PLANNED DATA SYNTHESIS**

**Strategy for data synthesis**
The odds ratios (ORs) will be calculated by the authors of the current review using the x² test. Heterogeneity in studies will be evaluated using the Q test and was quantified using I². An I² value <25% indicated homogeneity; 25% to just under 50%, low heterogeneity; 50% to just under 75%, moderate heterogeneity; and at least 75%, substantial heterogeneity. We planned to use a fixed-effects model to meta-analyze pooled data classified as homogeneous or low heterogeneity, and a random-effects model to meta-analyze data classified as showing moderate or substantial heterogeneity.

**Analysis of subgroups or subsets**
We will be performed the subgroup analysis based on intervention type and duration.
